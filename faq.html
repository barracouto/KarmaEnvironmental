Frequently Asked Questions

Why is stormwater runoff a problem?

Before flowing into our natural lakes, springs, and streams storm runoff collects many pollutants, debris, and even chemicals. Excess nutrients in runoff water will also negatively affect our water supply. Nutrients like nitrogen or phosphorus that are normally found in fertilizers can cause algal and bacterial blooms. Algae consumes oxygen and increases turbidity; killing fish and other aquatic life that need oxygen to survive.

What is a National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) permit?

The National Pollutant Discharge Elimination System (NPDES) Program was established by the Clean Water Act to regulate stormwater discharge. It is a federal program enacted to control any discharge of pollutants from a point source to our natural waterways.

Does my construction site need a NPDES permit?

If your construction site is greater than one acre, or part of a larger project that is greater than an acre you will need to obtain a NPDES permit and enact a stormwater prevention plan.

Does my industrial facility need a NPDES permit?

Some regulated operators may be eligible for an exclusion from having to obtain a NPDES permit. Realistically, most operators of regulated facilities will have to get a stormwater permit and implement a pollution prevention plan to reduce the pollutants of stormwater runoff.

What is a Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP)?

A Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP) is a written document that is signed by a company executive that specifies the activities and conditions at the site that can cause pollution and the steps that the facility is taking to prevent pollution to any natural body of water. Each SWPPP should include a site description, a pollution prevention team, activities that may cause pollution, control measures, a spill response plan, inspection and monitoring, and an employee training program.

What are best management practices (BMP)? Why are they important?

Best Management Practices (BMP) is a manual with a purpose of providing instruction on proper fertilization, pest control, irrigation, and cultural practices to any person who may be taking care of a lawn. These practices are important because they are designed to protect and preserve natural bodies of water from non-point-source pollutants that occur from agricultural fertilization.

What if I received a letter of non-compliance from a storm water regulatory body?

Immediately begin necessary corrective measures. When you respond to the letter make sure you include the cause of the violation, what the plan is to correct the violation, what corrective steps you have already taken, a specific timeline on when the corrections should be completed, and however you plan on preventing any future violations.
