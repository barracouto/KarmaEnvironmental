Serving the following industries:

Construction

Whether you are a homebuilder potentially disturbing over an acre, or a commercial residential developer or general contractor, your project may require you to apply for coverage under the Florida Construction Generic Permit (FCGP). Karma can assist with the Notice of Intent (NOI) process and submit to the Florida Department of Environmental Protection (FDEP) for each project. Before submitting an NOI to the State, Karma can create a site-specific Stormwater Pollution Prevention Plan (SWPPP).

Industrial

Whether you are managing a small manufacturing operation or an entire steam electric power plant, Karma is also ready to assist with any NOI or Notice of Termination (NOT) to use the NPDES Generic Permit for Discharges from Petroleum Contaminated Sites. If you have a large plant that will be using more than 50,000 gallons of water to discharge demineralized concentrate, as in either the concentrated byproduct water, brine, or reject water produced by ion exchange or membrane separation technologies such as reverse osmosis, membrane softening, ultrafiltration, membrane filtration, electrodialysis, and electrodialysis reversal used for desalination, softening, or reducing total dissolved solids during water treatment for public water supply purposes, in a 24-hour period you will need a permit to discharge demineralization concentrate. Karma environmental services is ready to help with that permit along with Wetlands permits and Discharge permits.

Municipal

Karma Environmental Services of Florida is focused on a local impact. We aim to not only serve communities throughout Florida, we pride ourselves on being an active partner within our communities. Through extraordinary leadership, industry expertise and local priorities, Karma of Florida has been able to show we are a reliable, privately owned, aide provider for permits that you need for your water and wastewater. If you discharge pollutants into a municipal storm sewer system, you may need a permit depending on what you discharge. NPDES permits establish discharge limits and conditions for discharges from municipal wastewater treatment facilities to Florida's waterways.
